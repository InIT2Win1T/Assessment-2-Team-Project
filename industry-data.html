Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why or why not?

 

Josh - After looking at the burning glass data, my opinion has massively changed. I have definitely learnt a lot about what kind of jobs are actually in demand and what jobs are not. As well as what skills are more valuable to employers compared to others, i was specifically surprised to see python so low, i thought because of its mainstream popularity it meant it was one of the most used programming languages. If i was to change what my ideal job was now i would probably switch from software engineer to software developer, because after looking at the data i feel like ‘developer’ is much more of the standard developing job compared to ‘engineer’, although i wouldn't rule it out in the future if i spent time as a successful developer.

 

Simon - According to the Burning Glass Data, software development, while still showing some active selections , was quite low compared to that of jobs such as solutions architects, graphic designers and .Net developers. Software engineering even, was even considerably higher up in terms of active selections. This has not yet driven me to make a drastic change in decision in terms of my ideal career. It has made me wonder more about the other higher demand positions that were mentioned and whether or not I should look into them more and what they involve. SQL, JavaScript and JAVA are highly sought after skills and ones that I would like to delve into more during my Information Technology degree. Baseline skills, such as communication (the highest sought after), problem solving, teamwork/collaboration and writing are skills that I have a reasonable degree of confidence in myself with. Organisational skills and leadership and skills I might need to work on a bit more.

 

Justin - Although Business Analyst is very low on the list of jobs according to demand, my ideal job has not changed.  There are 2 main reasons for this.

 

Firstly, because the skill set required for a Business Analyst still has many of the skills required in the IT industry. Looking at the chart for baseline skills, all of these are a requirement of a Business Analyst. The only possible exception would be mentoring. The fact that I have already developed all of the skills on this list in my current and previous roles means that I already have the baseline skill set required for any role in IT should I wish to change roles in the future. In the list of specialised skills there are also a number of skills that are required by a Business Analyst. These are SQL, Javascript, Windows, Project Management, Business Management, Building relationships, Business analysis, Technical support, C#, Customer service, Microsoft Office, scrum and business process. Again this indicates that the role of a Business Analyst has a lot of skills that can be transferred to other roles in the IT industry.

 

The second reason that my ideal job hasn’t changed is that the role of a Business Analyst is not limited to the IT industry. A Business Analyst is essentially a problem solver and solving a range of problems (not just IT problems) is very appealing to me.

 

Shang - According to Burning Glass data, cybersecurity and information security related occupations made up merely 5 percent of the top occupations in Information Technology for the period between March 2017 to February 2018. Based on Labour Insight Jobs for the aforementioned period, SQL, JavaScript and Java are the top 3 skills in greatest demand. Although there is no mention of any programming language requirement in his ideal job, Shang acknowledges the importance of coding in a cybersecurity context and wishes to learn SQL and JavaScript languages to possess the ability to recognize application vulnerabilities and to prevent possible exploits. Apart from IT specific skills, Shang realizes the importance of generic skills such as communication, problem solving, collaboration and troubleshooting which are ranked first, second, fifth and sixth in greatest demand from employers. Specific mention of keywords such as “provide consultation”, “continually improve”, “participate” and “periodic review” in Shang’s ideal job post can further corroborate the value of such attributes. There are specialized IT skills that Shang thinks are irrelevant to his ideal job. These include and are not limited to website production, graphic design and business analysis. As for top generic skills, Shang believes he does not need to possess skills such as mentoring, team building, presentation and research. Shang has not been swayed by going through Burning Glass data and remains assertive in his chosen path. Initially, Shang was astonished by how scarce the demand was back in 2018 but believes its demand has since been on the rise with more businesses shifting towards telecommuting in and post-pandemic.
