IT Work

 

Interview – David K – Business Analyst in the finance sector

 

Please tell us about your IT work. What exactly do you do?

 

I am a Business Analyst. I work with management and external providers to improve the way the business runs. Usually this would involve me designing and implementing a solution to an issue that has been raised but it also involves implementing other people’s ideas (which aren’t always the best) or acting in a purely advisory role.

 

 

Please tell us about the industry you work in.

 

I work in banking. Specifically I work in financial crime and fraud prevention. Our overall goal is obviously to work with our regulators to reduce the impacts of financial crime and fraud on the community, economy and our business.

 

 

What other kinds of work do you have to do?

 

At least a quarter of my week is given up to meetings. There are plenty of projects to keep me busy too but when I am not working on a project I would be running a training session on a new implementation.

 

 

What are some projects you are working on at the moment?

 

I can’t say too much but there is a LOT of change going on at the moment. Right now most of my attention is being put towards upgrading our Quality Assurance software so that all QA alerts can be actioned in the software and have results stored in a database rather than on an Excel spreadsheet. Work is forecast to increase a lot over the next few years so we are really trying to improve efficiency by improving the quality of the tools at our disposal.

 

 

Who are all the different people you interact with in your work? Please tell us about them.

 

Such a variety! The nature of my job means that I need to interact with managers at all levels including heads of departments, Subject Matter Experts, Consultants, other Business Analysts and external providers such as BAE Systems who I am working closely with at the moment. Consultants are the most important of these in my opinion. By consultants I mean the people that do the business as usual work such as entry level Risk Analysts or Customer Consultants. It is important to spend time with these people to gauge the potential impacts of any changes that I implement or recommend. This is something that is overlooked majorly by senior managers and other Business Analysts.

 

 

Please tell us about your interactions with other IT professionals.

 

I don’t spend a lot of time with what I would call IT professionals. I have spoken with consultants and software designers of external companies and I have found that I need to be very specific when discussing the needs of the business. I interact with other Business Analysts which is the closest to what you asked. They are generally good conversations and I love discussing our different ideas and points of view.

 

 

What about your interactions with clients or investors?

 

I don’t deal with investors at all. That is what the senior management is for! My clients would be the end users of our implementations as well as management. As long as the problem is solved management are usually happy. End users are harder to please but as I spoke about earlier I do try to identify all impacts of any changes before we work on them as I have been on the receiving end of poor decisions in the past and I can understand the frustrations that arise. One good thing about COVID is people can’t just walk up to my desk and tell me all their negative thoughts whenever they like.

 

 

What aspects of your work do you spend most time on? Please tell us about these.

 

Meetings, research and ideas and implementation. Meetings are the least fun and there are far too many. Sometimes an email is fine. I often find myself working on something else during meetings.

 

Research and ideas is the best part of my job and I have been able to learn an incredible amount about a lot of things like new skills online and I have also learnt a lot about the business through buddying with teams to learn what they do, why they do it and how they do it. How much time I spend on this depends how far into the project I am. In the early stages this would be at least half of my week (research is also the easiest thing to do during a meeting).

 

Implementation, similar to research and ideas, varies how much time I spend on it. Once the research is complete and I have a plan of attack I can start implementing it. Once implemented there is also tweaks and maintenance to be done. For example I spent a bit of time today adding some functionality to one of our tools so that when someone reports a fault they can’t leave the details blank. This tool released about 3 months ago so we are never truly finished with our work.

 

 

Which aspects of your work do you find most challenging?

 

The people. Whether it is someone senior that doesn’t listen or a software engineer that doesn’t understand the requirements. I am a very technical and introverted person so while I love my job, I find the more interpersonal things difficult.

 

 

Finally, can you share an example of the work you do that best captures the essence of the IT industry?

 

Well I don’t work in the IT industry so I am not really sure of the essence. I guess the problem solving side of things is what I would say is the essence of IT. Using technology to enhance the way that we live, work and play. An example of this was the creation of a bot which could produce a report on customer’s superannuation accounts based on the questions that financial advisers would ask when they called the call centre. This reduced the need for consultants to spend 15-30 minutes discussing an account on the phone when they could just email the documents. This need was identified by a consultant in the call centre and then  came to me for implementation. After this was implemented a whole new online suite was created for investors and advisers which demonstrates that the work is never really finished. I think this really demonstrates how a problem is identified and then IT professionals can provide a solution to the problem which in turn, through continuous improvement can be even more beneficial to the business.
