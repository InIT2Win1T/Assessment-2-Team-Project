Ideal Jobs

 

Each person in our team has chosen a different job as their ideal job. However, some of the jobs chosen are quite similar. For example, Shang would like to be a Cyber Security Analyst and Nick would like to be a Penetration Tester which is also in the realm of cyber security. Simon and Josh have both selected roles that involve building software and Justin has chosen a role as a Business Analyst. All of these roles require soft skills such as communication, teamwork and problem solving. Where these roles differ is in their specializations which are cyber security for Shang and Nick and programming for Simon and Joshua. Justin’s role does not have a specialized field but is a role that requires knowledge in a lot of areas (or good research skills) in order to provide the best solutions to the problems that have been identified by the business.

 

As the name of Shang and Nickolas’ jobs suggests these are primarily based around the security of IT networks. These roles include responsibilities such as data protection, securing and standardizing infrastructure and providing continuous monitoring of the effectiveness of the policies and procedures that are in use. While these roles are similar the key difference is that Shang would be implementing the security infrastructure and Nickolas would be trying to find weaknesses in the infrastructure and provide feedback to Shang to help improve the product.

 

Josh and Simon will need to build, deploy and maintain the platforms, systems, tools and environments required for his business to operate. The uses of these can range from inventory management to finance and user management to authentication. Continuous improvement is a requirement in this role in order to develop and support solutions that deliver a product that is easy to use.

 

In Justin’s role as a Business Analyst, he will be required to analyze business requirements through engagement with stakeholders and provide support to the business when implementing change. This role requires as much people skills as it does technical skills due to the business analyst being required to influence stakeholders in order to produce the best outcomes. The business analyst will provide recommendations for process improvements, assist with testing, training and implementation and undertake and support product releases.
